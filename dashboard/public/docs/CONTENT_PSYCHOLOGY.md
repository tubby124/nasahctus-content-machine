## 1. The TikTok Brain Framework

Every piece of short-form content lives or dies in 5 phases. Miss any one and the algorithm buries it.

### Phase 1: Pattern Interrupt (0-3 seconds)
- **71% of viewers decide to stay or leave in the first 3 seconds**
- **65% who make it past 3 seconds will watch 10+ seconds**
- **45% who pass 3 seconds will watch 30+ seconds**
- This is the ONLY phase that matters for distribution. Everything else is retention.

**What works:**
- Start mid-sentence ("So if you're living in Saskatoon right now...")
- Lead with a specific number ("$487,000. That's what this house sold for.")
- Ask a question that challenges assumptions ("You think you need 20% down?")
- Name the viewer ("First-time buyers in Calgary — stop scrolling.")

**What kills it:**
- Any form of greeting ("Hey guys!")
- Self-introduction ("I'm Hasan with eXp Realty")
- Throat-clearing ("So today I want to talk about...")
- Static text card with no motion or voice

### Phase 2: Value Confirmation (3-8 seconds)
- Confirm the promise of the hook
- Viewer is thinking: "Was the first 3 seconds worth it?"
- Deliver one clean sentence that proves value is coming

**Template:** "[Hook]. And what that means is [plain English impact on YOU]."

### Phase 3: Information Cascade (8-30 seconds)
- One fact per 5-7 seconds
- Each fact builds on the previous one
- Visual change (new overlay, graphic, position) every 5-7 seconds
- The brain needs novelty every ~7 seconds to maintain attention

**Hasan's natural rhythm:** Stat → translate → contrast → pivot question → insight. This IS the information cascade.

### Phase 4: Identity Moment (30-45 seconds)
- The viewer shifts from "this is interesting" to "this is for ME"
- Happens when content addresses their specific situation
- "If you're moving from Toronto to Calgary..."
- "For anyone renting in Saskatoon right now..."

### Phase 5: Single CTA (Final 5 seconds)
- ONE action only. Never two.
- Options: DM a keyword / Save this / Follow for part 2 / Link in bio
- Match CTA to content goal, not to vanity metrics

**CTA hierarchy by value:**
1. "DM me [KEYWORD]" → highest intent, builds pipeline
2. "Save this for when you need it" → saves = algorithm fuel
3. "Share this with someone moving to Calgary" → shares = reach
4. "Follow for part 2" → follow = compounding audience
5. "Link in bio" → lowest conversion, use sparingly

## 2. Lead Generation Psychology (EATA Funnel)

### Education → Authority → Trust → Action

**Education (Reels + Carousels):**
- Give away the "what" and the "how" freely
- The more you teach, the more they trust
- Specificity = credibility: "$487K" beats "almost $500K"
- "$3,500–$5,000 closing costs" beats "a few thousand dollars"

**Authority (Deal Stories + Data):**
- "Document, don't create" — share what you actually do, not what you think they want to hear
- Real deal stories are content-of-access that no other agent can replicate
- 11 offers on one court-appointed sale = PROOF of expertise
- 48 showings in 4 days = PROOF of market knowledge

**Trust (Consistency + Personality):**
- Trust builds through repeated exposure (mere exposure effect)
- 7-11 touchpoints before someone considers you their "go-to" agent
- Personality is the trust accelerator — people trust humans, not brands
- The "wasting money" joke does more for trust than 10 market updates

**Action (CTA + DM):**
- People DM when they feel a personal connection, not when they see a call to action
- The DM trigger is: "This person understands MY situation"
- Neighbourhood guides → "Which area should I look in?" (highest DM-generating content type)
- "Homes under $X" Reels → "Do you have more listings like this?" (second highest)
- Deal stories → "Can you help me do something similar?" (third highest)

### The "Helpful Friend" Frame

Position: You're the smartest friend they have who happens to sell real estate.

| Helpful Friend | Salesy Agent |
|---|---|
| "Here's what I'd actually tell a friend" | "Contact me for a free consultation" |
| "The math says..." | "It's a great time to buy" |
| "I've seen this mistake 3 times this month" | "Don't miss out on this opportunity" |
| "So yeah, peace" | "Like and subscribe for more" |
| Shares insider knowledge freely | Gates information behind "call me" |

## 3. Hook Science (Deep Dive)

### Formula 1: Pattern Interrupt
**Psychology:** Breaks the viewer's autopilot scrolling by presenting something unexpected.
**Mechanism:** Pattern recognition is the brain's default mode. Disrupting it creates a micro-moment of heightened attention.

**Real estate examples:**
- "Everyone moving to Calgary makes the same neighbourhood mistake."
- "This $215,000 house just sold for $487,000."
- "Your landlord is hoping you never see this math."

### Formula 2: Curiosity Gap
**Psychology:** The brain experiences mild discomfort with incomplete information (Zeigarnik effect). It NEEDS to close the loop.
**Mechanism:** Present an incomplete story. The viewer stays to get the resolution.

**Real estate examples:**
- "11 offers. The one that won wasn't the highest price."
- "There's one thing no one tells you about moving from Toronto to Calgary."
- "The #1 neighbourhood in Calgary has nothing to do with price."

### Formula 3: Direct Identity Address
**Psychology:** Name the viewer's identity and they feel personally spoken to (cocktail party effect — we hear our own name in a crowded room).
**Mechanism:** The viewer's brain shifts from "this is content" to "this is for me."

**Real estate examples:**
- "If you're moving from Toronto to Calgary, there's something nobody told you."
- "First-time buyers in Saskatoon — stop scrolling."
- "If you're renting and paying over $1,800/month in Calgary, this is your sign."

### Formula 4: Bold Contrarian Statement
**Psychology:** Challenges existing beliefs, creating cognitive dissonance that demands resolution.
**Mechanism:** The viewer either agrees (validation) or disagrees (engagement through comments). Both are wins.

**Real estate examples:**
- "Calgary's cheapest neighbourhood isn't where you think it is."
- "Stop waiting for prices to drop in Saskatoon. Here's why."
- "Open houses are a waste of your time. Here's what works instead."

### Formula 5: Stakes + Real Numbers
**Psychology:** Specific numbers activate the analytical brain and create a sense of tangible, real-world impact.
**Mechanism:** Vague claims feel like marketing. Specific numbers feel like insider access.

**Real estate examples:**
- "This property sold for $215,000 in 2019. It just sold for $487,000."
- "48 showings. 3 competing offers. They lost. Here's the exact mistake."
- "This $12,000 mistake costs Calgary first-time buyers every single month."

### Formula 6: Before/After Reveal
**Psychology:** Transformation narratives are hardwired into human storytelling instincts.
**Mechanism:** Show the gap between "before" and "after" — the viewer wants to be on the "after" side.

**Real estate examples:**
- "My client was paying $2,800/month rent in Toronto. Their mortgage in Calgary? $1,900."
- "This neighbourhood was overlooked 3 years ago. Here's what happened since."
- "Before: 48 showings, lost 3 offers. After: we changed one thing and won."

### Formula 7: Socratic Hook (from Mulrenin)
**Psychology:** Ask a question the viewer thinks they know the answer to, then reveal why the obvious answer is wrong.
**Mechanism:** Creates ego investment — the viewer stays to see if they were right.

**Real estate examples:**
- "What's the #1 thing that kills a real estate deal? It's not the price."
- "How much do you really need for a down payment in Alberta? Most people are way off."
- "What's the best neighbourhood in Calgary for investment? Your first guess is wrong."

### Batch Filming Protocol

**Schedule:** One 4-hour filming session every two weeks.
**Setup:** Ring light + phone on tripod. 3-4 outfit changes.
**Output target:** 15-20 videos per session.

**Process:**
1. Write 20 hooks the night before
2. Film each video in one take — imperfections are fine
3. Change outfit every 5 videos (visual variety)
4. Log each topic with planned posting date
5. Edit + add captions in CapCut (batch all editing in one session)
6. Schedule via Later or Meta Business Suite

## 7. Platform-Specific Signals

### Instagram Reels

| Signal | Weight | What It Means |
|---|---|---|
| Watch time | #1 factor | Total seconds watched |
| Sends/DM shares | Highest (for discovery) | Strongest signal for reaching NEW audiences |
| Saves | Very high | Long-term value signal |
| Likes/reach | High (for followers) | Content resonates with existing audience |
| Comments (quality) | Moderate | Multi-sentence > emoji |
| Originality | Gate | Reposted/watermarked content suppressed |

**Key stats:** 30.81% reach rate (2x other formats). 55% views from non-followers.

### TikTok

| Signal | Weight | What It Means |
|---|---|---|
| Completion rate | Highest | 70%+ threshold for distribution push |
| Rewatch rate | Critical | 15-20%+ = strong amplification |
| Shares | Very high | Share-to-view ratio > raw count |
| Comments | High | Reply threads especially |
| Likes | Moderate | Baseline, not differentiator |

**Mechanics:** New videos tested on 100-500 users first 3 hours. Under 15s = 92% avg completion.

### YouTube Shorts

| Signal | Weight | What It Means |
|---|---|---|
| Swipe-away rate | Highest (inverse) | Lower = better |
| Watch-through rate | Very high | % who watch to end |
| Replay count | High | Replays > CTR |
| Engagement rate | High | Relative to views |

### Algorithm Signal Ranking (Cross-Platform)

```
1. SAVES (IG) / REWATCHES (TikTok/YT) — "I want to see this again"
2. SHARES / SENDS — "Someone else needs to see this"
3. COMMENTS (substantive) — "This made me think"
4. COMPLETION RATE — "I watched the whole thing"
5. LIKES — "I approve of this"
```

**Implication:** Optimize for saves and shares, not likes. Ask "would someone save this?" before posting.

## Appendix: Hook Cheat Sheet

**Pattern Interrupts:**
1. "This [thing] costs $[amount]. Let me show you why."
2. "Your [landlord/bank/agent] doesn't want you to know this."
3. "I just [action] and I need to tell you what I found."

**Curiosity Gaps:**
4. "The #1 [thing] in [city] has nothing to do with [obvious answer]."
5. "There's one [detail] that [surprising outcome]."
6. "Most people think [belief]. They're wrong. Here's the data."

**Identity Address:**
7. "First-time buyers in [city] — stop scrolling."
8. "If you're renting and paying over $[amount]/month, this is for you."
9. "Every homeowner who [timeframe/action] needs to hear this."

**Contrarian:**
10. "The worst time to [action] is NOT when [common belief]."
11. "[Common advice] is actually terrible advice. Here's why."
12. "I tell my clients NOT to [action]. This works better."

13. "This $[amount] mistake costs [audience] every [timeframe]."
14. "One [tactic]. $[amount] saved. [Time] to learn."
15. "[X]% of [group] [negative outcome]. Here's how to be in the [100-X]%."
